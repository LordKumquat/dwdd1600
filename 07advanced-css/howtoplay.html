How to play Magic: the gathering

Reading a Magic card

  1. Card Name
    You can only have up to four cards of the same name in a single deck, except for Basic Lands, which are unlimited.
  2. Mana Cost
    Mana is used to cast spells. To cast a spell, you'll pay the colored mana plus additional mana of any color, for a total of the mana cost.
  3. Type
    Every card in Magic has a typre, such as Land, Creature, Artifact, Enchantment, Planeswalker, Instant, and Sorcery.
  4. Set Symbol
    The color of this symbol indicates rarity - black-and-white is common, silver is uncommon, gold is rare, and orange-gold is mythic rare (the rarest you can get).
  5. Text Box
    This is the large box underneath the picture is the box that contains the important information about what the card actually does.
  6. Power/Toughness
    This is the section of a card that exists only for creature spells and gives you the general size of the creature in question. These numbers are found in the bottom right corner of the card.

The colors of Magic

How many colors are in Magic?
There are currently 5 colors in Magic: the Gathering.
What are the colors of magic?
The 5 colors of magic include: White, Blue, Black, Red, and Green.
Why does the color matter?
Each color in magic has it's own unique identity, and it defines how the cards function, as well as their strengths and weaknesses.
